1. Scope & Structural Context

This section establishes the biochemical and structural parameters defining KRAS4B interactomics. Understanding the C-terminal hypervariable region (HVR) is critical for deciphering how KRAS4B recruits unique cytosolic chaperones and forms specific membrane nanoclusters compared to other RAS isoforms.

◉ KRAS4A vs KRAS4B

KRAS generates two splice variants. While identical in their G-domain (res 1-165), they diverge in the HVR. KRAS4A utilizes a palmitoylated cysteine alongside a farnesylated CAAX box. KRAS4B lacks palmitoylation; instead, it relies on a polybasic domain (PBD, lysines 175-180) adjacent to the farnesylated CAAX motif for electrostatic plasma membrane (PM) anchoring.

◉ The CAAX Box & Prenylation

The C-terminal CAAX motif (CVIM in KRAS4B) dictates farnesylation by FTase, cleavage by RCE1, and methylation by ICMT. CAAX deletion abolishes farnesylation, trapping KRAS4B in the cytosol, preventing interaction with the PM-trafficking chaperone PDE6D, and severing its ability to interact with PM-resident effectors like RAF1.

⚠ Oncogenic Alleles (G12D/V/C, Q61)

Mutations at Gly12, Gly13, and Gln61 primarily alter interaction kinetics rather than abolishing physical binding to effectors. However, they catastrophically impact GAP interactions.

  • → GAPs (NF1, p120GAP): Binding is transient or sterically hindered, preventing the catalytic arginine finger from entering the active site (PMID: 2128795).
  • → Effectors (RAF1, PI3K): Constitutively bound in the GTP-loaded state due to lack of hydrolysis, locking the signaling cascade 'ON'.
  • → A146T: Distinct from G12/Q61; accelerates intrinsic GDP/GTP exchange (GEF-independent activation) rather than blocking GAPs (PMID: 17290219).

5. Executive Interpretation

Core Interactome & Membrane Dependency

The most robustly supported KRAS4B interactors include classical effectors (RAF1, PIK3CA, RALGDS), upstream regulators (SOS1, NF1), and specific transport chaperones (PDE6D, CALM1). Interaction is strictly bipartite: PM-localization is mandatory for effector binding, while cytosolic chaperoning requires the unique, prenylated KRAS4B C-terminus.

CAAX-box deletion is catastrophic to the interactome. It completely severs interactions with PDE6D, traps the protein in the cytosol, and prevents the formation of nanoclusters required for high-avidity RAF1 dimerization.

Allelic Rewiring & Evolutionary Divergence

Oncogenic alleles (G12D, Q61H) do not invent new effector interactions; rather, they destroy negative regulation by sterically excluding GAPs (NF1, p120GAP), shifting the biochemical equilibrium to a constitutively 'ON' state.

While yeast S. cerevisiae Ras1/Ras2 share highly conserved regulatory mechanics (GEFs: CDC25/SDC25; GAPs: IRA1/IRA2) with human KRAS4B, the downstream effector biology is completely non-conserved. Yeast utilizes Ras to activate adenylate cyclase (CYR1) and cAMP signaling, completely lacking direct homologs for the primary human oncogenic effectors (RAF, PI3K). Thus, yeast is an excellent model for upstream KRAS regulation and prenylation, but useless for downstream MAPK/AKT pathway modeling.
